# Customer Hackathon Readiness Checklist

> A reusable checklist for planning a customer hackathon. Copy per engagement and check items off as they're confirmed with the customer.

## Checklist

- [ ] **Confirm dates** — event days and times.
- [ ] **Confirm room / venue** — space and capacity at the customer location.
- [ ] **Confirm audio / video** — AV setup, screen sharing, recording.
- [ ] **Confirm power / electricity** — enough outlets and power strips for every laptop (don't show up to 3 outlets for 100 laptops).
- [ ] **Confirm food** — meals and breaks.
- [ ] **Confirm Wi-Fi / network access** — guest network capacity for all laptops; access to GitHub/Copilot endpoints (firewall/proxy).
- [ ] **Confirm prerequisites / environment setup** — GitHub access, Copilot licenses active, required tools installed before arrival.
- [ ] **Confirm on-site point of contact** — facilities/host contact, building hours, and after-hours access (hack days can run late).
- [ ] **Confirm leadership involvement** — kick off the event and be available during Day 2 readouts.
- [ ] **Define how virtual teams work** — how remote participants join and collaborate (see suggestion below).
- [ ] **Distribute problem-statement PPTX** — send the customer the deck for teams to prepare and submit.
- [ ] **Confirm visitor check-in** — guest/badge process at the customer location.
- [ ] **Decide awards & judging criteria (optional)** — prizes for top teams and how winners are selected.
- [ ] **Finalize participant count** — final number of attendees.
- [ ] **Send Teams invitation to participants** — calendar invite with event details and join link.
- [ ] **Define how teams are formed** — pre-formed vs day-of.
- [ ] **Confirm Day 2 hacking projects** — should be developer-work related (see suggestions below).

## Suggestions

> These are options for the customer to decide — not requirements.

### Virtual teams

- One persistent virtual room per team; in-person members also join from their laptops so everyone collaborates digitally.
- A dedicated facilitator for virtual teams.
- Readouts presented via screen-share so remote teams aren't disadvantaged.

### Readout formats for groups over 100

A single readout per team works well up to ~100 people. For larger groups (more teams than fit in one readout), use one of these:

- **Heats → Finals** — split teams into parallel pods/rooms, each with its own judges; each pod sends 1–2 finalists to present to the full room.
- **Track-based readouts** — group teams by theme (e.g., tech-debt, developer productivity); each track runs its own readout and only track winners present on the main stage.

### How to choose Day 2 hacking projects

The customer decides these. Suggested sources for developer-work-related projects:

- Engineering managers nominate candidate projects.
- Tech-debt items.
- Backlog / sprint items.
- Developer-productivity improvements.
